About Me

I’m Md Parvez Uddin, and my graph didn’t shoot up overnight—it’s been climbing one careful step at a time. The first point on that curve was the moment I realized algorithms could do more than just pass exams—they could help doctors, patients, and real people. That idea pulled me into AI and ML during my B.Tech CSE at LPU and refused to let go.

Early on, my experiments were messy: models overfitting, code breaking, and concepts that only made sense at 2 a.m. after three attempts. Things shifted when I started working on medical AI projects like tuberculosis detection from chest X‑rays and disease prediction from patient symptoms; suddenly, the math on the screen felt connected to lives off the screen. Even exploring real‑time heart‑rate estimation with a phone camera made me see how AI could live in everyday devices.

Today, I’m deliberately sharpening my skills in applied ML, computer vision, and healthcare-focused AI, guided by a personal rule: never compromise integrity, always aim for meaningful impact. The next part of the curve for me is becoming an AI engineer who builds systems that are not just impressive—but genuinely useful to society

I enjoy working on data-driven solutions and collaborating on meaningful projects.
